Timeline of Significant Climate-related Events

1930's

Milutin Milankovitch publishes "Mathematical Climatology and Astronomical Theory of Climatic Changes" which explains the causes of the Earth's ice ages.












1960

A curve developed by American climate scientist Charles David Keeling begins to track atmospheric CO2 concentrations. CO2 concentration in 1960 ≈ 315 parts per million











1973

The first oil shock happens











1992

The United Nations conference in Rio de Janeiro creates the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change.











2006

China becomes the world’s largest GHG emitter.
